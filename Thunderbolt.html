Thunderbolt

What is Thunderbolt?

Thunderbolt is a type of serial connection protocol which combines the functionaily of PCIe and displayport, as well as being able to provide DC power. It was developed by and owned by Intel and from Thunderbolt 3 onwards use the same-shaped ports as USB-C and is mainly used to connect peripherals as most ports are. Thunderbolt is currently on its fouth iteration with the fifth planning to release sometime this year. Thunderbolt 4 has a maximum bitrate of 40 Gbits/s (equal to 5 Gigbytes per second) two ways, allowing it to be used to connect 2 4k Displays at 60Hz or one 4k display at 120 Hz. It can support at most 6 devices, it is useful for such purposes. While Thunderbolt is much faster than most USB-C, it is much more expensive.
